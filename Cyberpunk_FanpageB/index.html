Cyberpunk 2077 Lifepaths

In Cyberpunk 2077, your character's backstory is determined by your choice of lifepath. Each lifepath offers a unique starting point and influences your journey through Night City.

Street Kid

Born and raised in Night City's streets, you know them like the back of your hand. You've seen it all, from the glittering skyscrapers to the grimy back alleys.

As a Street Kid, you have a deep understanding of the city's underworld and its complex social dynamics. Your connections and street smarts give you an edge in navigating the dangerous waters of Night City.

Your journey begins in the heart of Heywood, where you're caught up in a job gone wrong. Your intimate knowledge of the streets and its people will be your greatest asset as you climb the ranks of Night City's criminal underworld.

Nomad

You're a child of the Badlands, born into a nomad clan that roams the vast deserts surrounding Night City. You've lived a life of freedom, but also one of hardship and constant movement.

As a Nomad, you bring a unique outsider's perspective to Night City. Your experience with machines and your strong family ties set you apart from the city-born inhabitants.

Your story begins as you're about to embark on a risky smuggling run into Night City. Your survival skills and adaptability will be put to the test as you navigate the unfamiliar urban jungle.

Corpo

You've climbed the corporate ladder and know the cutthroat world of big business in Night City. As a corporate executive for Arasaka, you've seen the inner workings of one of the most powerful megacorporations in the world.

As a Corpo, you possess insider knowledge of corporate politics and have access to high-level resources. Your understanding of power dynamics and corporate espionage gives you a unique advantage.

Your journey begins in the towering offices of Arasaka, where a high-stakes power play goes terribly wrong. Your corporate background will be both a blessing and a curse as you navigate the dangerous world outside the corporate bubble.
